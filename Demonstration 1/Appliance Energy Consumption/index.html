Energy Star Ratings

Australia's mandatory Energy Rating Labels help you compare appliance efficiency. The more stars, the less energy used and the cheaper to run.

Standby Power Costs

Appliances on standby mode can cost up to 10% of your electricity bill - over $100 annually. Switch devices off at the wall when not in use.

Understanding TV energy efficiency in the Australian market.

TV Energy Consumption in Australia

On average, a new television uses more energy than a fridge. Many Australian homes have multiple TVs, significantly increasing electricity costs.

Running Costs for 43-59 inch TVs (10 hours/day for 90 days)

  • LED TV: $18 - $81
  • LCD TV: $18 - $108
  • Plasma TV: $27 - $126

Understanding Energy Rating Labels

All TVs sold in Australia must display an Energy Rating Label showing:

  • Star Rating: Energy efficiency comparison (more stars = more efficient)
  • Energy Consumption: Estimated annual kWh usage

TVs must meet Minimum Energy Performance Standards and carry mandatory Energy Rating Labels.

Energy Saving Tips for TVs

  • Switch off TVs at the wall when not in use - standby mode still consumes power
  • Adjust brightness settings to reduce energy consumption
  • Choose TVs with high star ratings when purchasing
  • Use sleep timers to avoid leaving TVs on overnight
  • Consider screen size - larger screens typically use more energy
